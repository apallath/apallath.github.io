Overview

Biology happens in water, therefore understanding how the smallest components of biological systems interact with water at the nanoscale is essential to glean insight into the complex chemistry of life. At the Patel group, I use molecular simulations to study the effect of hydration on protein structure and interactions. My aim is to use the understanding we gain from these studies to help inform the design of future drugs and therapeutics.

I am also actively studying the effects of solvation on polymer conformation using molecular simulation and enhanced sampling algorithms. I hope to use the insights we gain from and the methods we develop for these studies to help guide my work on proteins.
