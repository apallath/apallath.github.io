About Me

I am a PhD candidate in Chemical and Biomolecular Engineering at the University of Pennsylvania. I am a member of the Patel Research Group at the Penn Institute for Computational Science since November 2019.

I study proteins at the nanoscale using high performance computing. The focus of my research is to predict the effect of hydration on protein structure, function, and intermolecular interactions. To do this, I use enhanced molecular modeling and simulation techniques developed or honed at the Patel group, combined with large scale data analysis and prediction algorithms. The simulation techniques we use at the Patel group (such as INDUS) allow us to simulate events that would take normally take several months to years of compute time on massively parallel clusters in the matter of hours to a few days.

I graduated from the Indian Institute of Technology (IIT) Gandhinagar in 2019 with a bachelors degree in Chemical Engineering with a minor in Computer Science and Engineering. My undergraduate research at IIT Gandhinagar, Caltech and JAIST included the study of interfacial fluctuations using molecular dynamics, heat transport in amorphous materials using molecular dynamics, performance benchmarking for density functional theory calculations, and some theoretical computer science research. I was also a competitive programmer, and participated in the ACM-ICPC India Regionals in 2017.

When I'm not busy reading papers, writing code or trying to make sense of my simulation results, I can be found playing the guitar, playing with my girlfriend's and my cat Dan-Dan, or (COVID-19 pending) trying out new restaurants and bars.
