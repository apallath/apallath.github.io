About me

I am a PhD student in Chemical and Biomolecular Engineering at the University of Pennsylvania, where I'm advised by Dr. Amish Patel. We are part of the Penn Institute for Computational Science. I conduct research at the interface of biology, chemistry, and computational science.

I am passionate about using computation to solve challenging problems. My graduate research is focused around studying protein hydration at an atomistic scale using a variety of computational tools. Prior to starting graduate school, I worked in research labs at Caltech, JAIST (Japan), and IIT Gandhinagar (India) on a variety of interdisciplinary problems in applied physics, materials science, chemistry, and computer science. These included the study of interfacial fluctuations using molecular dynamics (with Dr. Kaustubh Rane at IIT Gandhinagar), heat transport in amorphous materials using molecular dynamics (in Dr. Austin Minnich's lab at Caltech), performance benchmarking for density functional theory calculations (in Dr. Ryo Maezono's lab at JAIST), and theoretical computer science research in the field of computational social choice (with Dr. Neeldhara Misra at IIT Gandhinagar). I used computational tools and developed algorithms and frameworks - and in one instance, assembled parallel computing hardware - to solve these problems.

I was also an amateur competitive programmer, and participated in the ACM-ICPC India Regionals in 2017.

Research overview

Biology happens in water, therefore understanding how the smallest components of biological systems interact with water at the nanoscale is essential to glean insight into the complex chemistry of life. At the Patel group, I use high performance computing to study the effect of hydration on protein structure and interactions. My aim is to use the understanding we gain from these studies to help inform the design of future drugs and therapeutics.

Unfortunately, that's the most I can say about my research until it's published. Check back later for more information about what I've worked on, and its big-picture applications!
