About Me

I'm a PhD student in Chemical and Biomolecular Engineering at the University of Pennsylvania. I am a member of the Patel Research Group at the Penn Institute for Computational Science since November 2019. My research interests include the development and application of enhanced molecular simulation methods and deep learning methods for solving problems in computational biology. I currently study protein hydration and interactions using molecular dynamics simulations and umbrella sampling.

I graduated from the Indian Institute of Technology (IIT) Gandhinagar in August 2019 with a bachelors degree in Chemical Engineering with a minor in Computer Science and Engineering. My undergraduate research at IIT Gandhinagar, Caltech and JAIST included the study of interfacial fluctuations using molecular dynamics, heat transport in amorphous materials using molecular dynamics, performance benchmarking for density functional theory calculations, and also some theoretical computer science research.
