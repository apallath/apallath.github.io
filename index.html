About


I am a PhD candidate in Chemical and Biomolecular Engineering at the University of Pennsylvania, where I'm part of the Patel Group.

Research

My graduate research centers on characterizing the role of hydration in the conformational transitions of proteins, peptides, and polymers through the use of novel physics-informed approaches. This has diverse applications in the the fields of drug discovery, drug delivery and immunotherapy. I leverage the power of massively parallel computer simulations, enhanced sampling techniques, and rigorous data analysis frameworks to answer challenging research questions. A large part of my work involves writing scientific software, which is something that I greatly enjoy doing. For more details, check out my research and software pages.

Entrepreneurship and Consulting

Beyond graduate research, I am interested in using my scientific expertise and hypothesis-driven problem-solving skills to translate novel innovations from research labs to the market. I am involved in an early-stage entrepreneurial venture to commercialize technology developed in my research lab at Penn. As a part of this engagement, I participated in the National Science Foundation funded Penn I-Corps program, in which we conducted multiple customer discovery interviews to test our problem and product hypotheses. I am also working on pro-bono consulting engagements with clients in the healthcare industry through the Penn Biotech Group to gain experience in solving diverse business problems in the healthcare space.

Educational Background

Prior to starting my PhD, I obtained a Bachelor's degree in Chemical Engineering with a minor in Computer Science and Engineering from the Indian Institute of Technology (IIT) Gandhinagar. As an undergraduate, I worked in cutting-edge computational research labs at Caltech, JAIST, and IIT Gandhinagar on interdisciplinary problems in applied physics, materials science, chemistry, and computer science. I was also a competitive programmer, and participated in the ACM-ICPC India Regionals in 2017.
