About Me

I am a PhD student in Chemical and Biomolecular Engineering at the University of Pennsylvania. I am a member of the Patel Research Group, affiliated with the Penn Institute for Computational Science, since November 2019.

I study the effects of hydration on protein structure and function using high performance computing, to help inform the design of novel drugs and therapeutics. To do this, I use enhanced molecular modeling and atomistic simulation techniques developed or honed at the Patel group, combined with large scale data analysis and prediction algorithms. The simulation techniques we use at the Patel group (such as INDUS) allow us to simulate and gather statistics about events several orders of magnitude faster than traditional techniques. This allows us to study events which would traditionally take several months to years of compute time on massively parallel clusters in the matter of hours to a few days.

I graduated from the Indian Institute of Technology (IIT) Gandhinagar in 2019 with a bachelors degree in Chemical Engineering with a minor in Computer Science and Engineering. My undergraduate research at IIT Gandhinagar, Caltech and JAIST included the study of interfacial fluctuations using molecular dynamics, heat transport in amorphous materials using molecular dynamics, performance benchmarking for density functional theory calculations, and theoretical computer science research in the field of computational social choice. I was also a competitive programmer, and participated in the ACM-ICPC India Regionals in 2017.
